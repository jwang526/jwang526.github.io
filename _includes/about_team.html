Founders


Jayden Wang

Cellist and Pianist

Jayden Wang began his music journey at the age of four, when he began learning piano. After moving to Granger, Indiana, in 2011, he has participated in and won many regional and state competitions. He has studied piano under Linda Belleville. Jayden also began playing cello when he was 11. He has since performed as principal cellist in the Penn High School Symphonic Orchestra, the South Bend Youth Symphony Orchestras, and participated in a piano trio in the Fischoff National Chamber Music Competition, winning the Geoffroy Award. He currently studies cello under Lara Turner. Jayden was inspired to initiate Music For after performing a few times at local nursing homes. The experience for him was beneficial and enlightening, encouraging him to spread similar opportunities to as many people as possible.

Cameron Poznanski

Double Bassist


Cameron Poznanski, a senior at Penn High School, has aspired to be a great bassist since the sixth grade. He is the first in his family to fall in love with orchestral music and has taken huge steps in order to attain his aspirations. In his first year learning, with the help of his private teacher Gayle Sampson, he competed in the Level I ISSMA Solo Event, earning a gold medal with a renowned piece named The Elephant. Every year since, he has earned gold medals in both the ISSMA solo and ensemble events. It was Cam's second year of learning the double bass that he auditioned for the South Bend Youth Symphony, earning himself a spot in the highest orchestra, allowing him to get 1 on 1 help with Ed Randles, a bassist for the South Bend Symphony, every Sunday. Recently, Cam's talent was noticed when he was offered a spot to play with the Notre Dame Orchestra in their final concert Carmina Burana, and this summer, Cam has taken on the responsibility of teaching a middle school student to play the bass and hopefully inspire him to fall in love with the instrument like he did. Cam does not only have a passion for playing the bass, though, as he loves neuroscience, and he wishes to go to college to study it and find a career in the subject.

Suzana Josenhans

Violinist


Suzana Josenhans began playing violin as a three-year-old with the Suzuki program in Evansville, Indiana. Under the patient guidance of her private teacher, group lessons, and practicing, Suzana learned the fundamentals of violin technique and the art of listening. At the age of 10, Suzana began playing in the Evansville Philharmonic Youth Orchestra, where she served as concertmaster of the younger ensemble for the 2019-2020 season. She went on to play first violin in the large ensemble. Upon moving to South Bend, Indiana in July 2022, Suzana became a member of the Symphonic Orchestra at Penn High School and of the South Bend Youth Symphony Orchestra, serving in the first violin sections of both orchestras. She currently studies with Aviva Hakanoglu.

James Pennix

Violist


James's first experience in music was learning the piano around age 5. After he started learning piano, his parents bought a drum set for him and him siblings and he learned how to play it. Now, he plays the drum set for his church service every Sunday. He first began playing the viola when he was in 6th grade, at 11 years old.
